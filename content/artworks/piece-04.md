The Vulture and the Little Girl is a photo that captures the depths of the humanitarian crisis in Sudan during the 90s, when civil war and famine tore the country apart. The photo captures a child, now known to be a boy, trying to make his way to a UN aid station, collapsed on the trail. The photo managed to spark widespread support for Sudan and many rushed to donate to organizations that had operations there. The photo won the Pulitzer Prize for Feature Photography in 1994, and four months later, the photographer, Kevin Carter killed himself. We can only guess as to his motives in taking his own life, but it is not difficult to see how his work and experiences in Sudan could’ve played a major part. To this day this photo stands as a testament to the tragedy of famine, and the trauma those who document it face.
