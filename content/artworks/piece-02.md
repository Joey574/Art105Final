Paths of Glory by C.R.W Nevinson is a piece with quite an interesting background, it depicts two British soldiers from World War I lying dead on the ground. Forgotten, and unburied on the Western Front. The artist Nevinson, had been a volunteer ambulance driver for the Friends’ Ambulance Service during the opening months of the war, and eventually returned to London and served as an orderly, until he became too sick to continue. In 1917 he was sent to France by the British War Propaganda Bureau and began making paintings that depicted the horrors of the war, during this time he completed Paths of Glory. The painting was censored in France due to its depiction of allied corpses, despite this, Nevinson still included this piece in his exhibition, however, he put brown strips over the bodies, and on them read the word “censored”.
