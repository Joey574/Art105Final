This drawing, made by Kichisuke Yoshimura in 1974 after a call was issued by the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Museum and other entities for artwork. It depicts Hibakusha, survivors of the atomic bombings in Japan during the closing days of World War II. The figures depicted are haunting, and we can see how apparent the title is in the drawing, with the figures having ripped clothing and distorted skin. The artists recollects how he saw figures like the ones in his drawing down by a river, quietly moving towards the outskirts of town. He would describe these people as “Ghost-like”. Hiroshima and Nagasaki resulted in the immediate deaths of roughly 110k people, and in just a years time, would be linked to nearly another 100k. This drawing serves as a testament to the horror and wide-scale destruction of nuclear war.
