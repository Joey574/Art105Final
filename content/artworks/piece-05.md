Gassed is a haunting piece by John Sargent, an artist commissioned by the British Government to make a painting documenting World War I. In 1919 it was voted picture of the year by the Royal Academy of Arts and it’s not hard to see why. It is a truly massive canvas and the figures depicted in it are nearly life size, at the time gas attacks were something you’d hear about in the news, something that was always happening “over there”. To see the costs these attacks bring with them so vividly was a shock to many. Of course there’s not just the parade of blinded soldiers, but in the background a game of soccer being played can be seen. A shocking juxtaposition from the corpses in the foreground. From it we can gather just how desensitized these soldiers were, just how normal, and present these mountains of corpses felt.
